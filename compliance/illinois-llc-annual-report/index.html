Illinois LLC Annual Report: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 Â· Applies to: Illinois LLCs

Illinois requires every LLC to file an annual report with the Secretary of State. The report confirms your LLC's current information and keeps it in good standing. Illinois uses your LLC's formation anniversary month as the due date, not a fixed calendar date. The fee was reduced from $250 to $75 in 2024.

When is it due?

The Illinois annual report is due during the anniversary month of your LLC's formation, every year. The Secretary of State mails a reminder approximately 90 days before the due date. You can file up to 60 days before the due date.

How much does it cost?

The filing fee is $75. Illinois reduced this from $250 in 2024, making it significantly more affordable for small LLCs. There is no separate late fee, but failure to file will result in administrative dissolution.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to the Illinois Secretary of State business portal
Log in or search for your LLC by name or file number
Select the option to file your annual report
Review and update your LLC information
Pay the $75 filing fee
Save your confirmation

What happens if you don't file?

If you fail to file the annual report within the filing window, the Illinois Secretary of State will administratively dissolve your LLC. You can reinstate by filing the delinquent annual report(s) and paying all fees owed, plus a reinstatement fee.

Do you need a registered agent in Illinois?

Yes. Illinois requires every LLC to maintain a registered agent with a physical street address in Illinois. The registered agent receives service of process and official correspondence on behalf of your LLC.

Need a registered agent in Illinois?

A professional registered agent ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time and keeps your LLC in good standing.

Illinois LLC compliance checklist

Ongoing requirements for Illinois LLCs

File annual report during your anniversary month ($75)
Maintain a registered agent with an Illinois street address
File Illinois income tax return (Form IL-1065 for partnerships/multi-member LLCs)
File federal tax return (Form 1065 or Schedule C)
Collect and remit Illinois sales tax if applicable
Renew any local business licenses

Frequently asked questions

When did the filing fee drop to $75?

Illinois reduced the annual report filing fee from $250 to $75 effective in 2024. This applies to all LLCs filing annual reports from 2024 onward.

What if I miss my anniversary month?

If you miss your filing window, the Secretary of State will begin the dissolution process. File as soon as possible.

Can I file online?

Yes. Illinois strongly encourages online filing through the Secretary of State's business portal.
