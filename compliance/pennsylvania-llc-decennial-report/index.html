Pennsylvania LLC Decennial Report: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Pennsylvania LLCs

Pennsylvania is unique among US states — it does not require annual reports for LLCs. Instead, Pennsylvania requires a decennial report filed every 10 years. This makes Pennsylvania one of the lowest-maintenance states for LLCs. However, the infrequent filing schedule means many LLC owners forget about it entirely.

When is it due?

The decennial report is due every 10 years, during the year that ends in the same digit as the year your LLC was formed. For example, if your LLC was formed in 2018, your first decennial report is due in 2028. The report can be filed at any point during the decennial year.

How much does it cost?

The filing fee is $70. There is no late penalty, but failing to file may result in your LLC being listed as inactive in state records, which can create problems when applying for loans or entering contracts that require proof of good standing.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to the Pennsylvania Department of State website
Download or access the Decennial Report form
Complete the form with your LLC's current information
Submit the form and pay the $70 fee
Save your confirmation

What happens if you don't file?

Pennsylvania does not dissolve LLCs for failing to file the decennial report. However, your LLC may be flagged as inactive in state records. Banks and business partners may require proof of good standing that you cannot provide without filing. It is low-cost and easy to forget but worth staying on top of.

Do you need a registered agent in Pennsylvania?

Pennsylvania uses the term "registered office" instead of registered agent. Every Pennsylvania LLC must maintain a registered office address within the state. This can be your own business address or a commercial registered agent service.

Need a registered agent in Pennsylvania?

A professional registered agent ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time and keeps your LLC in good standing.

Pennsylvania LLC compliance checklist

Ongoing requirements for Pennsylvania LLCs

File decennial report every 10 years ($70)
Maintain a registered office with a Pennsylvania address
File Pennsylvania income tax return (PA-20S/PA-65 for pass-through entities)
File federal tax return (Form 1065 or Schedule C)
Collect and remit Pennsylvania sales tax if applicable
Renew any local business licenses

Frequently asked questions

Does Pennsylvania really not require annual reports?

Correct. Pennsylvania is one of the very few states that does not require annual or biennial reports. The decennial report every 10 years is the only recurring filing with the Department of State.

How do I know when my decennial report is due?

Look at the year your LLC was formed. Your report is due in every year ending in the same digit. Formed in 2016? Due in 2026, 2036, etc.

What if I never filed a decennial report?

You can file one now to bring your records current. There is no penalty for late filing, just the standard $70 fee.
