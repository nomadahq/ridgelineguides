Wyoming LLC Annual Report: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Wyoming LLCs

Wyoming LLCs must file an annual report each year. The minimum fee is $60 and is based on assets located in Wyoming. Wyoming has no state income tax, making the annual report one of the few compliance requirements.

When is it due?

The annual report is due by the first day of the anniversary month of your LLC formation.

How much does it cost?

The annual report fee is $60 minimum, or $60 per $250,000 of assets in Wyoming (whichever is greater). Most small LLCs pay the $60 minimum.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to Wyoming SOS WyoBiz
Search for your LLC
File the annual report
Pay the fee ($60 minimum)
Save confirmation

What happens if you miss the deadline?

A $50 late fee applies. After 60 days delinquent, the LLC may be administratively dissolved.

Do you need a registered agent in Wyoming?

Yes. Wyoming requires all LLCs to maintain a registered agent with a Wyoming address. This is especially important since many Wyoming LLCs are formed by out-of-state owners.

Need a registered agent in Wyoming?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Frequently asked questions

Does Wyoming have income tax?

No. Wyoming has no state income tax and no corporate income tax.

Why do people form LLCs in Wyoming?

No state income tax, strong privacy protections, low fees, and favorable LLC laws. However, you still need to register in your home state if you operate there.

Is the fee really just $60?

For most small LLCs with minimal assets in Wyoming, yes. The fee increases for LLCs with more than $250,000 in Wyoming assets.
