Washington LLC Annual Report: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Washington LLCs

Washington State requires every LLC to file an annual report with the Secretary of State. The report updates your LLC's information on file. Washington has no state income tax but does impose a Business & Occupation (B&O) tax on most businesses.

When is it due?

The Washington annual report is due by the last day of the month in which your LLC was formed.

How much does it cost?

The filing fee is $60. Washington does not charge a separate late fee, but failure to file will eventually lead to administrative dissolution.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to the Washington Secretary of State CCFS portal
Log in or search for your LLC
Select "Annual Report"
Review and update your LLC information
Pay the $60 filing fee
Save your confirmation

What happens if you don't file?

If you fail to file, the Secretary of State will send a delinquency notice. Continued non-filing results in administrative dissolution. Reinstatement requires filing all delinquent reports, paying fees, and filing a reinstatement application.

Do you need a registered agent in Washington?

Yes. Washington requires every LLC to maintain a registered agent with a physical street address in Washington State.

Need a registered agent in Washington?

A professional registered agent ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time and keeps your LLC in good standing.

Washington LLC compliance checklist

Ongoing requirements for Washington LLCs

File annual report by your anniversary month ($60)
Maintain a registered agent with a Washington street address
Pay Washington B&O (Business & Occupation) tax — applies to most businesses
File federal tax return (Form 1065 or Schedule C)
Collect and remit Washington sales tax if applicable

Frequently asked questions

Does Washington have a state income tax?

No. But Washington imposes a Business & Occupation (B&O) tax on gross receipts, which applies to most businesses.

What is the B&O tax?

The B&O tax is based on gross receipts, not net income. Rates range from 0.471% to 1.5% depending on business activity.

Can I file by mail?

Yes, but online filing through the CCFS portal is faster and provides immediate confirmation.
