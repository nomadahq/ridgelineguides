Arkansas LLC Annual Franchise Tax: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Arkansas LLCs

Arkansas LLCs must file a Franchise Tax Report and pay $150 annually. The deadline is May 1. This is filed with the Secretary of State, not the Department of Finance and Administration.

When is it due?

The Franchise Tax Report is due May 1 each year. The report can be filed online starting January 1.

How much does it cost?

The franchise tax is a flat $150 per year for LLCs. Late fees are $25 per month, up to a maximum penalty of $200.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to Arkansas SOS
Search for your LLC
File the Franchise Tax Report
Pay the $150 fee
Save confirmation

What happens if you miss the deadline?

Late filing incurs a $25/month penalty up to $200. Continued non-filing leads to revocation of good standing.

Do you need a registered agent in Arkansas?

Yes. Arkansas requires all LLCs to maintain a registered agent with a street address in Arkansas.

Need a registered agent in Arkansas?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Frequently asked questions

Is the franchise tax based on revenue?

No. It is a flat $150 for LLCs regardless of revenue or activity.

Can I file online?

Yes. File through the Arkansas Secretary of State website.

What if I just formed my LLC?

You must file starting the year after formation. The first report is due May 1 of the year following your LLC formation.
