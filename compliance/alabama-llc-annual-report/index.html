Alabama LLC Annual Report: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Alabama LLCs

Alabama LLCs must file a Business Privilege Tax Return annually. This functions as both the annual report and a tax filing. The minimum tax is $100. The return is due April 15, aligned with federal tax deadlines.

When is it due?

The Business Privilege Tax Return is due April 15 each year, the same deadline as federal taxes. Extensions are available.

How much does it cost?

The minimum privilege tax is $100 for LLCs with net worth up to $200,000. The tax increases based on net worth at a rate of $1.75 per $1,000 of net worth.

How to file

Filing steps

Calculate your LLC net worth
Go to My Alabama Taxes
File the Business Privilege Tax Return
Pay the privilege tax ($100 minimum)
Save confirmation

What happens if you miss the deadline?

Late filing results in penalties and interest. Continued non-filing can lead to administrative dissolution of the LLC.

Do you need a registered agent in Alabama?

Yes. Alabama requires all LLCs to maintain a registered agent with a physical address in the state. The registered agent receives legal and state documents on your behalf.

Need a registered agent in Alabama?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Frequently asked questions

Is the Business Privilege Tax the same as an annual report?

Essentially yes. Alabama combines its annual reporting requirement with the Business Privilege Tax Return.

What if my LLC had no income?

You still owe the $100 minimum privilege tax regardless of income or activity.

Can I file online?

Yes. File through My Alabama Taxes at myalabamataxes.alabama.gov.
