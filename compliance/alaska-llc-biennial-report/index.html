Alaska LLC Biennial Report: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Alaska LLCs

Alaska LLCs must file a biennial report every two years with the Division of Corporations. The filing fee is $100. Alaska has no state income tax, making the biennial report the primary state compliance requirement.

When is it due?

Your biennial report is due January 2 of your assigned filing year. Whether you file in odd or even years depends on when your LLC was formed.

How much does it cost?

The biennial report filing fee is $100. There is no additional tax or assessment.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to Alaska PRIOR database
Search for your LLC
File the biennial report online
Pay the $100 filing fee
Save confirmation

What happens if you miss the deadline?

Failure to file the biennial report will result in involuntary dissolution of the LLC. Reinstatement requires filing all overdue reports plus fees.

Do you need a registered agent in Alaska?

Yes. Alaska requires all LLCs to maintain a registered agent with a physical street address in Alaska.

Need a registered agent in Alaska?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Frequently asked questions

Does Alaska have a state income tax?

No. Alaska has no state income tax, making the biennial report one of very few compliance requirements.

How do I know if I file in odd or even years?

Check your filing with the Division of Corporations. Your assigned year depends on your formation date.

Can I file online?

Yes. File through the Alaska Corporations database.
