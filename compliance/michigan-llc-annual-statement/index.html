Michigan LLC Annual Statement: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Michigan LLCs

Michigan requires every LLC to file an annual statement with LARA (Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs). At $25, Michigan is one of the most affordable states. Michigan uses a universal February 15 deadline for all LLCs, unlike most states that use formation anniversaries.

When is it due?

The Michigan annual statement is due by February 15 of each year. This is a universal deadline — all LLCs file by the same date regardless of formation month.

How much does it cost?

The filing fee is $25. There is no separate late fee, but failure to file will eventually lead to administrative dissolution.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to the Michigan LARA Corporations Online Filing System
Search for your LLC by name or ID number
Select the annual statement filing option
Review and update your LLC information
Pay the $25 filing fee
Save your confirmation

What happens if you don't file?

If you miss February 15, your LLC is not immediately dissolved. However, continued non-filing will trigger the administrative dissolution process. File as soon as possible if you miss the deadline.

Do you need a registered agent in Michigan?

Yes. Michigan requires every LLC to maintain a resident agent (Michigan's term for registered agent) with a physical Michigan street address.

Need a registered agent in Michigan?

A professional registered agent ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time and keeps your LLC in good standing.

Michigan LLC compliance checklist

Ongoing requirements for Michigan LLCs

File annual statement by February 15 ($25)
Maintain a resident agent with a Michigan street address
File Michigan income tax return (Form MI-1065 for partnerships/multi-member LLCs)
File federal tax return (Form 1065 or Schedule C)
Collect and remit Michigan sales tax if applicable

Frequently asked questions

What changed in 2023?

Michigan renamed the "annual report" to "annual statement" for LLCs formed after January 1, 2023. The requirements are the same.

Why is the deadline in February?

Michigan uses a universal February 15 deadline for all LLCs rather than individual formation dates.

Can I file after February 15?

Yes, you can file late. There is no immediate penalty, but prolonged non-filing leads to dissolution.
