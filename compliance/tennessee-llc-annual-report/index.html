Tennessee LLC Annual Report: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 Â· Applies to: Tennessee LLCs

Tennessee requires every LLC to file an annual report with the Secretary of State. At a $300 minimum fee for LLCs with 6 or fewer members, Tennessee is one of the more expensive states for LLC maintenance. The fee increases to $50 per member for larger LLCs.

When is it due?

The Tennessee annual report is due by the first day of the fourth month after your LLC's fiscal year ends. For calendar-year LLCs, this means April 1.

How much does it cost?

The minimum filing fee is $300 for LLCs with 6 or fewer members. For LLCs with more members, the fee is $50 per member with no maximum cap.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to the Tennessee Secretary of State portal
Search for your LLC by name or control number
Select the annual report filing option
Review and update your LLC information
Pay the filing fee ($300 minimum)
Save your confirmation

What happens if you don't file?

Failure to file results in penalties and may lead to administrative dissolution. Dissolved LLCs must go through reinstatement, filing all delinquent reports and paying all fees and penalties.

Do you need a registered agent in Tennessee?

Yes. Tennessee requires every LLC to maintain a registered agent with a physical street address in Tennessee.

Pay Tennessee franchise and excise tax if applicable
File federal tax return (Form 1065 or Schedule C)

Frequently asked questions

Why is Tennessee's fee so high?

Tennessee's $300 minimum is simply the legislatively set fee. For LLCs with more than 6 members, it increases to $50 per member.

Does Tennessee have a state income tax?

Tennessee does not tax wages/salaries, but imposes a franchise and excise tax on business entities.

What is the franchise and excise tax?

The franchise tax is based on net worth (0.25%, minimum $100) and the excise tax is based on net income (6.5%). Both are filed with the Department of Revenue.
