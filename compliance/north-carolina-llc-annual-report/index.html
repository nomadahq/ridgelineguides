North Carolina LLC Annual Report: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 Â· Applies to: North Carolina LLCs

North Carolina requires every LLC to file an annual report with the Secretary of State. The report updates the state on your LLC's current information including its registered agent, principal office, and members or managers. It is a straightforward online filing that takes about 10 minutes.

When is it due?

The North Carolina annual report is due by April 15 of each year. The filing period opens January 1. The Secretary of State sends email reminders if you have an email address on file.

How much does it cost?

The filing fee is $200 regardless of whether you file on time or late. However, if you fail to file by the due date, an additional $200 penalty is assessed, bringing the total to $400.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to the NC Secretary of State annual report portal
Search for your LLC by name or SOS ID number
Review and update your LLC information (registered agent, addresses, members/managers)
Pay the $200 filing fee by credit card or e-check
Save your confirmation receipt

What happens if you don't file?

If you fail to file the annual report, the North Carolina Secretary of State will administratively dissolve your LLC. Dissolved LLCs lose their legal standing and liability protections. You can apply for reinstatement, but you must file all delinquent annual reports and pay all outstanding fees plus penalties.

Do you need a registered agent in North Carolina?

Yes. North Carolina requires every LLC to maintain a registered agent with a physical street address in North Carolina. The registered agent receives legal documents and official state correspondence on your behalf.

Need a registered agent in North Carolina?

A professional registered agent ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time and keeps your LLC in good standing.

North Carolina LLC compliance checklist

Ongoing requirements for North Carolina LLCs

File annual report by April 15 ($200)
Maintain a registered agent with a North Carolina street address
File North Carolina income tax return (Form D-403 for partnerships/multi-member LLCs)
File federal tax return (Form 1065 or Schedule C)
Collect and remit North Carolina sales tax if applicable
Renew any local business licenses or privilege licenses

Frequently asked questions

Can I file the North Carolina annual report by mail?

Yes, North Carolina accepts both online and paper filings. However, online filing is faster and provides immediate confirmation.

What if my LLC was just formed this year?

If your LLC was formed during the current calendar year, your first annual report is due the following April 15.

Is the $200 fee tax deductible?

Generally, yes. State filing fees for maintaining your business entity are typically deductible as a business expense. Consult your tax professional.
