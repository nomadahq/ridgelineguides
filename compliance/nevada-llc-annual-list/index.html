Nevada LLC Annual List: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Nevada LLCs

Nevada requires every LLC to file an Annual List of Managers or Managing Members with the Secretary of State, plus a separate State Business License renewal. Combined, these cost $350 per year — making Nevada more expensive than many business owners expect, despite its reputation as a tax-friendly state.

When is it due?

The Nevada annual list is due by the last day of the month in which your LLC was formed. The state business license renewal is due at the same time.

How much does it cost?

The annual list filing fee is $150. The state business license fee is $200. Combined total: $350 per year. Late filing of the annual list triggers a $75 penalty.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to the Nevada Secretary of State annual list page
Search for your LLC by entity number or name
File the Annual List and update your managers/members information
Pay the $150 annual list fee
Renew your State Business License ($200)
Save your confirmation

What happens if you miss the deadline?

If you fail to file the annual list, a $75 late penalty is added. Continued non-filing results in your LLC being revoked and placed in default status. You must apply for reinstatement and pay all outstanding fees, penalties, and reinstatement costs.

Do you need a registered agent in Nevada?

Yes. Nevada requires every LLC to maintain a registered agent with a physical Nevada street address.

Need a registered agent in Nevada?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Frequently asked questions

Is Nevada really tax-free for LLCs?

Nevada has no state income tax, no franchise tax, and no personal income tax. However, the annual list ($150) and business license ($200) cost $350/year — more than many states with income taxes charge in filing fees. The Commerce Tax applies to businesses with Nevada gross revenue exceeding $4 million.

What is the Commerce Tax?

A tax on Nevada gross revenue exceeding $4 million annually. Rates vary by industry. Most small LLCs are well below the threshold.

Why is Nevada so expensive for a "tax-friendly" state?

Nevada compensates for its lack of income tax with higher filing fees. The $350 annual cost surprises many LLC owners who chose Nevada for tax reasons.
