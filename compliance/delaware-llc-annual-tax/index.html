Delaware LLC Annual Tax: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Delaware LLCs

Delaware LLCs must pay an annual tax of $300 by June 1 each year. There is no annual report required for LLCs in Delaware — just the tax payment. Delaware is popular for formations, but the $300 annual tax applies to all domestic LLCs regardless of activity.

When is it due?

The annual tax is due June 1 each year. There is no report to file — just a payment.

How much does it cost?

The annual tax is a flat $300 for all LLCs. Late payment incurs a $200 penalty plus 1.5% monthly interest on the unpaid amount.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to Delaware Division of Corporations
Navigate to annual tax payment
Enter your LLC file number
Pay the $300 annual tax
Save confirmation

What happens if you miss the deadline?

Late payment results in a $200 penalty plus 1.5% monthly interest. Non-payment leads to forfeiture of good standing and potential voiding of the LLC.

Do you need a registered agent in Delaware?

Yes. Delaware requires all LLCs to maintain a registered agent with a physical address in Delaware. This is especially important since most Delaware LLCs do not operate in Delaware.

Need a registered agent in Delaware?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to file an annual report?

No. Delaware LLCs do not have an annual report. You only need to pay the $300 annual tax.

Why is Delaware so popular for LLCs?

Delaware has a well-established business court (Court of Chancery), privacy protections, and flexible LLC laws. However, the $300 annual tax applies to all LLCs.

What if my LLC has no activity?

You still owe the $300 annual tax regardless of business activity.
