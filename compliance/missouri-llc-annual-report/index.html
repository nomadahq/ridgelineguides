Missouri LLC Annual Report: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Missouri LLCs

Missouri does not require LLCs to file an annual report. This makes Missouri one of the most business-friendly states for ongoing compliance. However, you must still maintain a registered agent and keep your information current.

When is it due?

Missouri does not require an annual report for LLCs. This is unusual — most states require some form of annual or biennial filing.

How much does it cost?

Since there is no annual report, there is no annual fee.

How to file

Filing steps

No annual filing required
Maintain your registered agent
Update your information if it changes via Missouri SOS
File any required amendments as needed

What happens if you miss the deadline?

Since there is no annual report, there is nothing to miss. However, failure to maintain a registered agent can still affect your LLC standing.

Do you need a registered agent in Missouri?

Yes. Even though Missouri has no annual report, you must still maintain a registered agent with a Missouri address.

Need a registered agent in Missouri?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Frequently asked questions

Does Missouri really have no annual report?

Correct. Missouri is one of the few states with no annual report or fee for LLCs.

Do I still need a registered agent?

Yes. A registered agent is required regardless of the annual report requirement.

Are there any annual fees at all?

No state annual fees. You may still have local business license fees depending on your city or county.
