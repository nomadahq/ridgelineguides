Colorado LLC Periodic Report: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 Â· Applies to: Colorado LLCs

Colorado requires every LLC to file a periodic report with the Secretary of State. At just $10, Colorado has one of the lowest filing fees in the country. The report confirms your LLC's current information.

When is it due?

The Colorado periodic report is due during a 3-month window that begins in your LLC's anniversary month. For example, if formed in March, your window runs March 1 through May 31.

How much does it cost?

The filing fee is $10, making Colorado one of the most affordable states for LLC maintenance.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to the Colorado Secretary of State business portal
Search for your LLC by name or ID number
Select "File Periodic Report"
Review and update your LLC information
Pay the $10 filing fee
Save your confirmation

What happens if you don't file?

If you fail to file within the 3-month window, your LLC will be marked as delinquent. Continued delinquency leads to administrative dissolution. You can reinstate by filing the delinquent report and paying the fee.

Do you need a registered agent in Colorado?

Yes. Colorado requires every LLC to maintain a registered agent with a physical street address in Colorado.

Need a registered agent in Colorado?

A professional registered agent ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time and keeps your LLC in good standing.

Colorado LLC compliance checklist

Ongoing requirements for Colorado LLCs

File periodic report during your 3-month window ($10)
Maintain a registered agent with a Colorado street address
File Colorado income tax return (Form 106 for partnerships)
File federal tax return (Form 1065 or Schedule C)
Collect and remit Colorado sales tax if applicable

Frequently asked questions

Why does Colorado call it a periodic report?

Colorado uses the term "periodic report" instead of "annual report," but it serves the same purpose and is filed annually.

Is $10 really the full cost?

Yes. Colorado's filing fee is $10 with no additional state fees for LLCs beyond this annual filing.

What does delinquent status mean?

Your LLC has missed its filing deadline. It still exists but is not in good standing until you file.
