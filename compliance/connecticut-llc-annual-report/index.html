Connecticut LLC Annual Report: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Connecticut LLCs

Connecticut LLCs must file an annual report with the Secretary of the State. The report is due by the anniversary of your LLC formation each year. The filing fee is $80.

When is it due?

Your annual report is due by the anniversary month of your LLC formation each year.

How much does it cost?

The annual report fee is $80. A $50 late fee applies to late filings.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to CONCORD system
Log in or search for your LLC
File the annual report
Pay the $80 fee
Save confirmation

What happens if you miss the deadline?

A $50 late fee applies. Continued non-filing can lead to administrative dissolution.

Do you need a registered agent in Connecticut?

Yes. Connecticut requires all LLCs to maintain a registered agent (called a statutory agent) with a Connecticut address.

Need a registered agent in Connecticut?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Connecticut LLC compliance checklist

Ongoing requirements for Connecticut LLCs

File annual report by anniversary date
Pay $80 filing fee
Maintain registered agent in Connecticut
Report any changes to members or address

Frequently asked questions

When exactly is my anniversary date?

It is the month and day your LLC was originally formed or registered in Connecticut.

Can I file online?

Yes. File through the CONCORD system on the Secretary of State website.

What happens if my LLC is dissolved?

You will need to file for reinstatement, pay all overdue fees, and file all missed reports.
