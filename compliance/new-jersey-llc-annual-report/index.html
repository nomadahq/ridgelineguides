New Jersey LLC Annual Report: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: New Jersey LLCs

New Jersey requires every LLC to file an annual report with the Division of Revenue and Enterprise Services. The $75 fee is moderate compared to neighboring states. The report updates your LLC information and keeps it in good standing.

When is it due?

The New Jersey annual report is due by the anniversary of your LLC formation. The Division of Revenue sends reminders, but it is your responsibility to file on time.

How much does it cost?

The filing fee is $75. Late filing may result in additional penalties and can eventually lead to revocation of your LLC certificate.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to the New Jersey Division of Revenue portal
Search for your LLC by name or entity ID
Select the annual report filing option
Review and update your LLC information
Pay the $75 filing fee
Save your confirmation

What happens if you miss the deadline?

If you fail to file, the Division of Revenue may revoke your LLC certificate. A revoked LLC cannot legally conduct business in New Jersey. Reinstatement requires filing all delinquent reports and paying all outstanding fees and penalties.

Do you need a registered agent in New Jersey?

Yes. New Jersey requires every LLC to maintain a registered agent with a physical New Jersey street address.

Need a registered agent in New Jersey?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

New Jersey LLC compliance checklist

Ongoing requirements for New Jersey LLCs

File annual report by formation anniversary ($75)
Maintain a registered agent with a New Jersey street address
File New Jersey partnership return (NJ-1065) if multi-member LLC
File federal tax return (Form 1065 or Schedule C)
Collect and remit New Jersey sales tax if applicable
Renew any local business licenses

Frequently asked questions

What happens if my certificate is revoked?

A revoked LLC cannot legally conduct business in New Jersey. You must apply for reinstatement, file all delinquent reports, and pay all outstanding fees.

Can I file online?

Yes. New Jersey allows online filing through the Division of Revenue portal.

Is New Jersey expensive for LLCs?

At $75 per year, New Jersey is moderate. It is cheaper than neighbors New York ($9 biennial but with expensive publication requirement) and Massachusetts ($500).
