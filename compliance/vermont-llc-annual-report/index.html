Vermont LLC Annual Report: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Vermont LLCs

Vermont LLCs must file an annual report each year. The fee is $35. The deadline is within the quarter of your LLC formation anniversary.

Quick summary

ItemDetails
FilingAnnual Report
FrequencyEvery year
Due dateWithin the anniversary quarter of formation
Filing fee$35
Late feeAdministrative dissolution after non-filing
Where to fileVermont Secretary of State (sos.vermont.gov)
Consequence of non-filingAdministrative dissolution

When is it due?

The annual report is due within the calendar quarter of your LLC formation anniversary.

How much does it cost?

The annual report fee is $35.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to Vermont SOS Corporations
Search for your LLC
File the annual report
Pay the $35 fee
Save confirmation

What happens if you miss the deadline?

Failure to file leads to administrative dissolution.

Do you need a registered agent in Vermont?

Yes. Vermont requires all LLCs to maintain a registered agent with a Vermont address.

Need a registered agent in Vermont?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Frequently asked questions

What is the anniversary quarter?

The 3-month period containing your LLC formation date. E.g., formed in February = Q1 (Jan-Mar) deadline.

Can I file online?

Yes. File through the Vermont Secretary of State website.

What information is required?

Registered agent, principal address, and member information.
