Ohio LLC Biennial Report: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Ohio LLCs

Ohio requires LLCs to file a biennial report with the Ohio Secretary of State every two years. The best part: there is no filing fee. Ohio is one of the few states where this required filing is completely free.

When is it due?

The Ohio biennial report is due by the anniversary date of your LLC's formation, every two years. Whether you file in odd or even years depends on when your LLC was formed.

How much does it cost?

There is no filing fee for the Ohio biennial report. It is completely free to file online.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to the Ohio Secretary of State business portal
Search for your LLC by name or charter number
Select the option to file your biennial report
Confirm or update your LLC's information
Submit — no payment required
Save your confirmation

What happens if you don't file?

If you fail to file the biennial report, the Ohio Secretary of State will send a notice. If you miss two consecutive filing periods (4 years), the Secretary of State may cancel your LLC. Reinstatement requires filing all delinquent reports and paying a reinstatement fee.

Do you need a registered agent in Ohio?

Yes. Ohio requires every LLC to designate a statutory agent (Ohio's term for registered agent) with a physical Ohio street address.

Ohio LLC compliance checklist

Ongoing requirements for Ohio LLCs

File biennial report every 2 years ($0 — free)
Maintain a statutory agent with an Ohio street address
File Ohio income tax return (IT-1040 for individuals, IT-4708 for pass-through entities)
Pay Ohio Commercial Activity Tax (CAT) if gross receipts exceed $150,000
File federal tax return (Form 1065 or Schedule C)
Collect and remit Ohio sales tax if applicable

Frequently asked questions

Is the Ohio biennial report really free?

Yes. Ohio does not charge a filing fee. It is one of the most affordable states for LLC maintenance.

What is the Commercial Activity Tax?

The Ohio CAT is a tax on gross receipts for businesses with more than $150,000 in Ohio gross receipts. It is separate from the biennial report.

What happened to the Statement of Continued Existence?

Ohio renamed this filing to "biennial report" to align with terminology used by most other states.
