Kansas LLC Annual Report: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Kansas LLCs

Kansas LLCs must file an annual report by April 15 each year with the Secretary of State. The filing fee is $55. Kansas uses a tax-day-aligned deadline.

When is it due?

The annual report is due April 15 each year, matching the federal tax deadline.

How much does it cost?

The filing fee is $55. Late filing incurs a $75 penalty.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to Kansas SOS Business Center
Search for your LLC
File the annual report
Pay the $55 fee
Save confirmation

What happens if you miss the deadline?

A $75 penalty applies for late filing. Two consecutive missed reports result in forfeiture of your LLC organization.

Do you need a registered agent in Kansas?

Yes. Kansas requires all LLCs to have a resident agent with a Kansas street address.

Need a registered agent in Kansas?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Frequently asked questions

What happens if I miss two years?

Your LLC can be forfeited after two consecutive missed annual reports.

Is the deadline always April 15?

Yes, regardless of when your LLC was formed.

Can I file online?

Yes. File through the Kansas Secretary of State business center.
