Indiana LLC Biennial Report: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Indiana LLCs

Indiana requires every LLC to file a biennial report (called a Business Entity Report) with the Secretary of State every two years. The online filing fee is $32, making Indiana one of the more affordable states for LLC maintenance.

When is it due?

The Indiana biennial report is due during the anniversary month of your LLC formation, every two years. The Secretary of State sends reminders by mail or email.

How much does it cost?

The filing fee is $32 when filed online through INBiz, or $50 for paper filing. There is no separate late fee, but non-filing leads to dissolution.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to Indiana INBiz portal
Create an account or log in
Search for your LLC and select the biennial report
Review and update your LLC information
Pay the $32 filing fee (online)
Save your confirmation

What happens if you miss the deadline?

If you fail to file, the Indiana Secretary of State will send a notice and may administratively dissolve your LLC. Reinstatement requires filing the delinquent report and paying all outstanding fees.

Do you need a registered agent in Indiana?

Yes. Indiana requires every LLC to maintain a registered agent with a physical Indiana street address.

Need a registered agent in Indiana?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Indiana LLC compliance checklist

Ongoing requirements for Indiana LLCs

File biennial report every 2 years ($32 online)
Maintain a registered agent with an Indiana street address
File Indiana income tax return (Form IT-65 for partnerships)
File federal tax return (Form 1065 or Schedule C)
Collect and remit Indiana sales tax if applicable
Renew any local business licenses

Frequently asked questions

Why is it cheaper online?

Indiana incentivizes online filing through INBiz by charging $32 vs $50 for paper. Online is also faster.

What is INBiz?

INBiz is Indiana's online business portal where you can file reports, update information, and manage your LLC.

When is my first report due?

Your first biennial report is due during the anniversary month of your LLC formation, two years after formation.
