Massachusetts LLC Annual Report: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Massachusetts LLCs

Massachusetts requires every LLC to file an annual report with the Secretary of the Commonwealth. At $500, Massachusetts has one of the highest annual report filing fees in the entire country. The report updates the state on your LLC current information.

When is it due?

The Massachusetts annual report is due by the anniversary date of your LLC organization.

How much does it cost?

The filing fee is $500, making Massachusetts one of the most expensive states for this requirement. There is no discount for early filing.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to the Massachusetts Secretary of the Commonwealth website
Search for your LLC and access the annual report filing
Review and update your LLC information
Pay the $500 filing fee
Save your confirmation

What happens if you miss the deadline?

Failure to file may result in administrative dissolution. Reinstatement requires filing all delinquent reports and paying all outstanding fees and penalties.

Do you need a registered agent in Massachusetts?

Yes. Massachusetts requires every LLC to maintain a resident agent with a physical Massachusetts street address.

Need a registered agent in Massachusetts?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Massachusetts LLC compliance checklist

Ongoing requirements for Massachusetts LLCs

File annual report by formation anniversary ($500)
Maintain a resident agent with a Massachusetts street address
File Massachusetts income tax return (Form 3)
File federal tax return (Form 1065 or Schedule C)
Collect and remit Massachusetts sales tax if applicable
Renew any local business licenses

Frequently asked questions

Why is the fee so expensive?

Massachusetts simply charges a higher fee than most states. It is the legislatively set fee — one of the highest in the country.

Can I reduce the fee?

No. The $500 fee is a flat fee that applies to all LLCs regardless of size or revenue.

Is the fee deductible?

Generally, yes. State filing fees are typically deductible as a business expense. Consult your tax professional.
