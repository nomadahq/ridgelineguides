Maryland LLC Annual Report and Personal Property Tax Return: Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Maryland LLCs

Maryland requires every LLC to file an Annual Report / Personal Property Tax Return with the State Department of Assessments and Taxation (SDAT). This is a combined filing — it updates your LLC information and reports personal property holdings. At $300, Maryland is one of the more expensive states for LLC maintenance.

When is it due?

The Maryland annual report is due by April 15 of each year. This is a universal deadline for all Maryland entities.

How much does it cost?

The filing fee is $300. Late filing results in additional penalties. The fee applies regardless of whether your LLC has personal property to report.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to Maryland Business Express
Log in or create an account
Search for your LLC and select the annual report filing
Complete the personal property section (most small LLCs report zero)
Update LLC information if needed
Pay the $300 filing fee
Save your confirmation

What happens if you miss the deadline?

Failure to file by April 15 triggers penalties. Continued non-filing can result in forfeiture of your LLC charter, meaning your LLC loses its legal standing. Reinstatement requires filing all delinquent reports and paying all outstanding fees and penalties.

Do you need a registered agent in Maryland?

Yes. Maryland requires every LLC to maintain a registered agent with a physical Maryland street address.

Need a registered agent in Maryland?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Maryland LLC compliance checklist

Ongoing requirements for Maryland LLCs

File annual report / personal property tax return by April 15 ($300)
Maintain a registered agent with a Maryland street address
File Maryland income tax return (Form 510 for pass-through entities)
File federal tax return (Form 1065 or Schedule C)
Collect and remit Maryland sales tax if applicable
Renew any local business licenses

Frequently asked questions

Why does the annual report include a personal property tax return?

Maryland combines both into a single filing. Most small service-based LLCs report no personal property, but you must still complete the form. Personal property means business equipment, furniture, and inventory — not real estate.

Is the $300 fee deductible?

Generally, yes. State filing fees are typically deductible as a business expense. Consult your tax professional.

Can I file online?

Yes. Maryland Business Express allows online filing and payment.
