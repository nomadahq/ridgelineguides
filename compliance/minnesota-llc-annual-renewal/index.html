Minnesota LLC Annual Renewal: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Minnesota LLCs

Minnesota LLCs must file an annual renewal by December 31 each year. Minnesota charges no fee for the annual renewal, making it one of the simplest compliance requirements.

Quick summary

ItemDetails
FilingAnnual Renewal
FrequencyEvery year
Due dateDecember 31
Filing feeNo fee (free)
Late feeAdministrative dissolution after non-filing
Where to fileMinnesota Secretary of State (sos.state.mn.us)
Consequence of non-filingAdministrative dissolution

When is it due?

The annual renewal is due December 31 each year.

How much does it cost?

Minnesota charges no fee for the LLC annual renewal.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to Minnesota SOS Business Portal
Search for your LLC
File the annual renewal
No payment required
Save confirmation

What happens if you miss the deadline?

Failure to file leads to administrative dissolution. Reinstatement requires filing overdue renewals.

Do you need a registered agent in Minnesota?

Yes. Minnesota requires all LLCs to maintain a registered agent (called registered office) in Minnesota.

Need a registered agent in Minnesota?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Frequently asked questions

Is there really no fee?

Correct. Minnesota does not charge for LLC annual renewals.

What information is required?

Registered office address, name of registered agent, and principal office address.

Can I file online?

Yes. File through the Minnesota Secretary of State business portal.
