Oregon LLC Annual Report: Deadline, Cost, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Oregon LLCs

Oregon LLCs must file an annual report each year by their formation anniversary. The filing fee is $100.

Quick summary

ItemDetails
FilingAnnual Report
FrequencyEvery year
Due dateBy the anniversary of LLC formation
Filing fee$100
Late feeAdministrative dissolution after continued non-filing
Where to fileOregon Secretary of State (sos.oregon.gov)
Consequence of non-filingAdministrative dissolution

When is it due?

Your annual report is due by the anniversary of your LLC formation each year.

How much does it cost?

The annual report fee is $100.

How to file

Filing steps

Go to Oregon SOS Business Registry
Search for your LLC
File the annual report
Pay the $100 fee
Save confirmation

What happens if you miss the deadline?

Continued non-filing leads to administrative dissolution. Reinstatement requires filing overdue reports and paying fees.

Do you need a registered agent in Oregon?

Yes. Oregon requires all LLCs to maintain a registered agent with an Oregon address.

Need a registered agent in Oregon?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Frequently asked questions

Does Oregon have sales tax?

No. Oregon has no state sales tax, but does have state income tax.

Can I file online?

Yes. File through the Oregon Secretary of State business registry.

What is the deadline?

Your LLC formation anniversary date each year.
