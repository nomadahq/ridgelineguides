Texas LLC Franchise Tax: Deadline, No-Tax-Due Threshold, and Filing Guide (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Texas LLCs, LLPs, corporations, and other taxable entities

Texas does not have a traditional state income tax, but it does have a franchise tax — sometimes called the Texas margin tax. Every LLC registered or doing business in Texas must file a franchise tax report annually, even if the LLC owes no tax. Failing to file can result in penalties and eventually lead to the forfeiture of your LLC's right to do business in Texas.

Who has to file?

Every taxable entity formed in Texas or doing business in Texas must file a franchise tax report. This includes LLCs, LLPs, corporations, partnerships, professional associations, and business trusts. Sole proprietorships (not registered as LLCs) are exempt.

Even if your LLC earned zero revenue or falls below the no-tax-due threshold, you must still file a report. The filing requirement is separate from the tax obligation.

The no-tax-due threshold

If your LLC's annualized total revenue is at or below $2.47 million, you owe no franchise tax. However, you must still file a No Tax Due Report and a Public Information Report (PIR) with the Texas Comptroller by the deadline.

Most small LLCs owe $0 in franchise tax. The $2.47 million threshold means that the vast majority of small businesses and single-member LLCs will not owe any tax. But you still must file the report — failure to file triggers penalties regardless of whether tax is owed.

When is it due?

The Texas franchise tax report is due May 15 of each year. The report covers the prior year's accounting period. For example, the report due May 15, 2026 covers the accounting period ending in 2025.

You can request an extension to file by the original due date. The extension gives you until November 15. However, if you owe any tax, you must pay the estimated amount by May 15 even if you extend the filing date.

How much does it cost?

RevenueTax owed
$2.47 million or less (annualized)$0 (file No Tax Due Report)
Above $2.47 million (retail/wholesale)0.375% of taxable margin
Above $2.47 million (all other businesses)0.75% of taxable margin

There is no filing fee for the franchise tax report itself. The cost is the tax owed, if any.

How to file

Filing steps for LLCs below the no-tax-due threshold

Go to the Texas Comptroller website
Log in to your Webfile account (or create one if this is your first filing)
File the No Tax Due Report (Form 05-163)
File the Public Information Report (Form 05-102) with current ownership and address information
Submit both forms by May 15
Save your confirmation for your records

What happens if you do not file?

If you fail to file the franchise tax report by the deadline (or extended deadline), the Texas Comptroller will:

  • Assess a $50 penalty for late filing
  • Add a 5% penalty on any tax owed if paid within 30 days of the due date, or 10% if paid after 30 days
  • Charge 1.5% interest per month on unpaid tax
  • Eventually forfeit your LLC's right to do business in Texas
Forfeiture is serious. If your LLC's right to transact business is forfeited, each officer or director becomes personally liable for debts incurred after the forfeiture date. Your liability protection disappears until you reinstate.

First-year filing (initial report)

If your LLC was formed during the current year, your first franchise tax report is typically due the following May 15. However, you will also receive an initial franchise tax questionnaire shortly after forming your LLC. Complete and return this questionnaire — it is not the annual report but it establishes your account with the Comptroller.

Do you need a registered agent in Texas?

Yes. Texas requires every LLC to maintain a registered agent with a physical street address in Texas. The registered agent receives service of process and official state correspondence on your behalf.

Texas LLC compliance checklist

Annual requirements for Texas LLCs

File franchise tax report by May 15 (No Tax Due Report if under $2.47M revenue)
File Public Information Report (PIR) by May 15
Maintain a registered agent with a Texas street address
Collect and remit sales tax if selling taxable goods or services in Texas
Renew any local permits or business licenses (varies by city and county)
File federal tax returns (Form 1065 for multi-member LLCs, Schedule C for single-member LLCs)

Frequently asked questions

Does Texas have a state income tax?

Texas does not have a personal state income tax. However, the franchise tax (margin tax) applies to business entities including LLCs. It functions as a business-level tax on entities with revenue above the no-tax-due threshold.

Can I file by mail?

The Texas Comptroller encourages electronic filing through the Webfile system. Paper forms are available but electronic filing is faster and provides immediate confirmation.

What if my LLC had no revenue?

You must still file. Submit the No Tax Due Report and the Public Information Report by May 15. There is no cost, but failure to file triggers penalties.
