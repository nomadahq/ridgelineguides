Gusto vs Paychex (2026): Payroll for Small Business Compared

Last updated: February 2026

Gusto is the modern, transparent payroll option. Paychex is the established player with broader HR services. Like ADP, Paychex does not publish pricing.

Quick verdict

Choose Gusto for transparent pricing and modern self-service. Choose Paychex if you need extensive HR, compliance, and insurance bundled with payroll.

Gusto: Pros and cons

Pros

  • Transparent pricing
  • Modern interface
  • Easy self-service setup
  • Full-service payroll with tax filing
  • Good integrations

Cons

  • Less scalable for very large businesses
  • Fewer HR features at enterprise level
  • Limited international payroll
  • Support can be slow at peak periods

Paychex Flex: Pros and cons

Pros

  • Scales from 1 to thousands of employees
  • Extensive HR and compliance
  • Dedicated support rep on higher tiers
  • PEO option for outsourced HR
  • Insurance services bundled

Cons

  • No public pricing
  • Dated interface
  • Sales process required for setup
  • Inflexible contracts
  • Aggressive upselling

Who should use Gusto?

Gusto is better for small businesses under 100 employees that want simple, transparent payroll.

Who should use Paychex Flex?

Paychex is better for businesses needing bundled HR, compliance, insurance, or PEO services.

The bottom line

For most small businesses under 50 employees, Gusto is simpler and more transparent. Paychex offers more breadth for larger or more complex organizations. The lack of published pricing from Paychex pushes many small businesses toward Gusto.

Frequently asked questions

Why no published pricing from Paychex?

Paychex uses custom quoting based on company size and features. This allows price discrimination but frustrates comparison shoppers.

Can I switch mid-year?

Yes, but time it at the start of a quarter. Your new provider needs year-to-date payroll data.

Does Gusto handle contractors?

Yes. Gusto pays both W-2 employees and 1099 contractors and handles form generation.
