Mailchimp vs ConvertKit (2026): Which Email Platform Fits Your Business?

Last updated: February 2026

Mailchimp and ConvertKit are both popular email marketing platforms, but they serve different audiences. Mailchimp is a broad marketing platform with email, landing pages, social ads, and e-commerce features. ConvertKit is built specifically for creators — bloggers, podcasters, course creators, and anyone who sells digital products. This guide compares them honestly.

Quick verdict

Choose Mailchimp if you run an e-commerce store or want an all-in-one marketing platform with design flexibility. Choose ConvertKit if you are a creator, blogger, or course seller who values simple automation and subscriber-first features.

Mailchimp: Pros and cons

Pros

  • All-in-one marketing platform (email, ads, landing pages, social)
  • Strong e-commerce integrations (Shopify, WooCommerce)
  • More email design templates and customization
  • Broader feature set beyond email
  • Established brand with large user base

Cons

  • Pricing can get expensive as contact list grows
  • Contact-based pricing means you pay for unsubscribed contacts on some plans
  • Automation is less intuitive than ConvertKit
  • Interface has gotten cluttered over time
  • Free plan is limited (500 contacts)

ConvertKit: Pros and cons

Pros

  • Subscriber-centric model — one record per person, no duplicate charges
  • Superior automation for sequences and funnels
  • Sell digital products directly (no separate platform needed)
  • Clean, focused interface
  • Excellent email deliverability reputation

Cons

  • Fewer email design options (more text-focused)
  • Limited features outside of email
  • Smaller template library
  • Less suitable for e-commerce product businesses
  • Creator Pro pricing can add up with large subscriber counts

Who should use Mailchimp?

Mailchimp is better for e-commerce businesses, small businesses that want one platform for email, social ads, and landing pages, and anyone who values email design flexibility. It is the broader marketing platform.

Who should use ConvertKit?

ConvertKit is better for creators, bloggers, podcasters, course creators, and anyone selling digital products. Its subscriber-first model, clean automation, and built-in digital product sales make it the best choice for creator businesses.

The bottom line

If you sell physical products or need a broad marketing platform, Mailchimp is the better fit. If you are a creator who sells digital products, courses, or memberships and values clean automation, ConvertKit is purpose-built for you. Both are solid — the right choice depends on your business model, not which one is "better" in the abstract.

Frequently asked questions

Which has better deliverability?

ConvertKit has a strong reputation for deliverability, partly because it encourages plain-text-style emails that perform well in inboxes. Mailchimp deliverability is good but can vary depending on your sending practices.

Can I sell products with ConvertKit?

Yes. ConvertKit has a built-in Commerce feature that lets you sell digital products, subscriptions, and courses directly — no need for a separate platform like Gumroad or Teachable.

Is Mailchimp still good for small businesses?

Yes, but it has gotten more expensive and complex over the years. For simple email needs, ConvertKit or even free alternatives may be more appropriate. Mailchimp shines when you use its full marketing suite.
