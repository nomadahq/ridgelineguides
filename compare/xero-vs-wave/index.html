Xero vs Wave (2026): Paid vs Free Accounting Software

Last updated: February 2026

Xero costs $15+/month. Wave is free. Both offer double-entry accounting, bank feeds, and invoicing. This guide helps you decide whether Xero is worth paying for.

Quick verdict

Choose Xero if you need multi-currency, unlimited users, inventory, or robust reporting. Choose Wave for straightforward accounting at zero cost.

Xero: Pros and cons

Pros

  • Unlimited users
  • Multi-currency
  • Inventory tracking
  • 1,000+ integrations
  • Project tracking

Cons

  • Costs money
  • Starter plan limited
  • Learning curve
  • Less dominant in US

Wave: Pros and cons

Pros

  • Completely free
  • Genuinely functional
  • No user limits
  • No feature gates
  • Easy to use

Cons

  • No multi-currency
  • No inventory
  • No project tracking
  • Fewer integrations
  • Email-only support

Who should use Xero?

Xero is worth it for international clients, product businesses, or teams needing deep integrations.

Who should use Wave?

Wave is right for freelancers and small service businesses with simple domestic accounting needs.

The bottom line

Wave is genuinely good enough for many businesses at zero cost. Xero justifies its price when you need features Wave lacks. Start with Wave. Move to Xero when complexity demands it.

Frequently asked questions

Is Wave going to stay free?

Wave has been free since founding. It monetizes through payment processing and payroll.

Can I migrate from Wave to Xero?

Yes. Export from Wave, import to Xero with some manual cleanup.

Can my accountant use Wave?

Some do, but QuickBooks and Xero are more common.
