Squarespace vs Wix (2026): Which Website Builder Is Better?

Last updated: February 2026

Squarespace is known for design quality. Wix is known for flexibility. Both let you build a professional website without coding — but the experience is very different.

Quick verdict

Choose Squarespace if design quality and brand aesthetics matter most. Choose Wix if you want maximum flexibility and more templates.

Squarespace: Pros and cons

Pros

  • Consistently beautiful templates
  • Every site looks professional
  • Strong blogging
  • Clean interface
  • All-in-one pricing

Cons

  • Less design flexibility
  • Fewer templates
  • Smaller app marketplace
  • No phone support
  • Cannot switch templates after launch

Wix: Pros and cons

Pros

  • Maximum flexibility
  • Huge template library
  • Extensive app marketplace
  • Free plan available
  • Phone support
  • AI website builder

Cons

  • Easier to make an ugly site
  • Cannot switch templates after publishing
  • Separate mobile editor
  • Free plan has branding
  • Can feel cluttered

Who should use Squarespace?

Squarespace is better for creatives, photographers, restaurants, and portfolios that value polished design.

Who should use Wix?

Wix is better for small businesses wanting flexibility, more features, and a free starting point.

The bottom line

Squarespace produces more beautiful websites. Wix gives more flexibility. Choose aesthetics (Squarespace) or options (Wix).

Frequently asked questions

Which is better for SEO?

Both have solid SEO tools. Content quality matters more than builder choice.

Can I build an online store?

Yes on both. Squarespace is more streamlined; Wix has more apps.

Can I switch builders later?

Yes but it requires rebuilding. Choose carefully.
