FreshBooks vs Wave (2026): Paid vs Free Accounting Software Compared

Last updated: February 2026

FreshBooks is a paid accounting platform starting at $19/month. Wave is completely free for accounting and invoicing. The question most freelancers and small business owners ask is simple: is FreshBooks worth paying for when Wave does the basics for free? This guide breaks down exactly what you get with each.

Quick verdict

Choose FreshBooks if you send a lot of invoices, need time tracking, or want a polished client-facing experience. Choose Wave if you are bootstrapping, have simple accounting needs, and want to spend $0 on software.

FreshBooks: Pros and cons

Pros

  • Polished, professional invoicing experience
  • Built-in time tracking and project profitability
  • Automated payment reminders reduce late payments
  • Multi-currency support
  • Responsive customer support including phone

Cons

  • Costs money ($19–$60/month)
  • No inventory tracking
  • Limited reporting compared to QuickBooks/Xero
  • User limits on lower plans

Wave: Pros and cons

Pros

  • Completely free for accounting and invoicing
  • No user limits
  • Genuinely functional double-entry accounting
  • No trial period or feature gates
  • Good enough for simple businesses

Cons

  • No time tracking
  • No project profitability tracking
  • No multi-currency support
  • Email-only customer support
  • Fewer integrations
  • Payment processing fees are slightly higher

Who should use FreshBooks?

FreshBooks is worth paying for if invoicing is a core part of your business, you need time tracking, you work with international clients (multi-currency), or you value responsive customer support. The automation features (payment reminders, recurring invoices) can pay for themselves if they reduce your late payments.

Who should use Wave?

Wave is the right choice if you are just starting out, have a very tight budget, and need basic accounting and invoicing without paying anything. It is genuinely useful — not a stripped-down demo. If your needs are simple (track income, track expenses, send occasional invoices), Wave handles it.

The bottom line

Wave is one of the few truly free accounting tools that is actually good enough for real use. If you are bootstrapping and every dollar matters, start with Wave. If your business relies heavily on invoicing, client management, or time tracking, FreshBooks is worth the cost — the time you save and the late payments you avoid will likely exceed the subscription fee.

Frequently asked questions

Is Wave really free? What is the catch?

Wave is genuinely free for accounting and invoicing. It makes money from payment processing fees (2.9% + $0.60 per credit card transaction) and its optional payroll add-on. There are no hidden feature gates or trial periods.

Can I switch from Wave to FreshBooks later?

Yes. You can export your data from Wave and import it into FreshBooks. It is easier to switch before you have extensive transaction history.

Which is better for tax time?

Both generate profit and loss reports and other tax-ready reports. FreshBooks has slightly more detailed tax reporting. Neither replaces a tax professional for filing.
