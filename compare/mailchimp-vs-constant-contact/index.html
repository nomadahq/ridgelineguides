Mailchimp vs Constant Contact (2026): Email Marketing Compared

Last updated: February 2026

Mailchimp and Constant Contact are two of the longest-running email marketing platforms. Mailchimp has evolved into a broader marketing platform. Constant Contact focuses on email marketing and event management.

Quick verdict

Choose Mailchimp for broader marketing with advanced automation and e-commerce. Choose Constant Contact for simpler email marketing with event management.

Mailchimp: Pros and cons

Pros

  • Free plan available
  • Advanced automation
  • Better e-commerce integrations
  • Social ad management
  • Larger feature set

Cons

  • Gets expensive as list grows
  • Interface has gotten complex
  • Limited support on free plan
  • Strict compliance policies

Constant Contact: Pros and cons

Pros

  • More email templates (200+)
  • Built-in event management
  • Phone support on all plans
  • Simpler to learn
  • Better for non-profits

Cons

  • No free plan
  • Less advanced automation
  • Fewer features beyond email
  • Can feel dated

Who should use Mailchimp?

Mailchimp is better for e-commerce, digital marketers, and anyone wanting email as part of a broader platform.

Who should use Constant Contact?

Constant Contact is better for local businesses, non-profits, and organizations that host events.

The bottom line

Mailchimp is more powerful and versatile. Constant Contact is simpler and more approachable. Choose based on whether you need advanced automation or straightforward email with great support.

Frequently asked questions

Which is better for beginners?

Constant Contact has a gentler learning curve and phone support.

Does Constant Contact have a free plan?

No. 60-day trial only. Mailchimp has a free plan for 500 contacts.

Which is better for events?

Constant Contact by a wide margin — built-in event management with invitations, RSVPs, and tickets.
