New York CPA License Renewal: CPE Hours and Deadline (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: New York LLCs

New York CPAs must register every three years with the State Education Department. New York requires 40 hours of CPE per year (not per cycle), making it one of the more demanding CPE states. The registration fee is $100 per 3-year cycle.

When is it due?

Your registration expires every 3 years from issuance. The State Education Department sends renewal notices.

How much does it cost?

The registration renewal fee is $100 per 3-year cycle. CPE costs are additional.

How to file

Filing steps

Complete 40 hours CPE per year (120 total for 3-year cycle)
Go to NYSED website
Submit registration renewal
Pay $100 fee

What happens if you miss the deadline?

Expired registration means you cannot practice. Reinstatement requires filing all delinquent information and paying fees.

Do you need a registered agent in New York?

CPE Requirements: 40 hours per year (not per cycle). Must include: 4 hours ethics every 3 years, with the remainder in subjects related to accounting practice. At least 24 of the 40 annual hours must be in technical subjects.

Need a registered agent in New York?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Retain CPE records for 5 years

Frequently asked questions

Why does New York require 40 hours per year?

New York has one of the highest CPE requirements in the country. This reflects the state board's emphasis on ongoing professional competency.

Can I carry over excess hours?

No. New York requires 40 hours each calendar year. Excess hours do not carry forward.

Is the license and registration the same thing?

No. You hold a CPA license (permanent) and a registration (renewed every 3 years). You need both to practice.
