Florida Contractor License Renewal: CE Hours, Deadline, and Costs (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Florida LLCs

Florida contractor licenses are renewed biennially through the DBPR. Renewal requires 14 hours of continuing education including mandatory courses on building codes, workplace safety, and Florida laws.

When is it due?

All Florida contractor licenses expire August 31 of even-numbered years (e.g., 2026, 2028). This is a universal deadline.

How much does it cost?

Renewal fees vary by license type but are approximately $209 for certified contractors. CE costs are separate.

How to file

Filing steps

Complete 14 hours of CE before August 31
Go to myfloridalicense.com
Submit renewal application
Pay the renewal fee
Save confirmation

What happens if you miss the deadline?

Late renewal is possible with additional fees and potentially additional CE hours. Extended non-renewal requires reinstatement application.

Do you need a registered agent in Florida?

CE Requirements: 14 hours per 2-year cycle including: 1 hour workplace safety, 1 hour business practices, 1 hour Florida laws and rules, 1 hour workers comp, 1 hour building codes, and elective hours.

Need a registered agent in Florida?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Frequently asked questions

When is the next renewal deadline?

August 31, 2026 for the current cycle.

Can I take CE online?

Yes. Florida accepts online CE from approved providers.

What if I am a registered (not certified) contractor?

Registered contractors renew through their local jurisdiction rather than the state DBPR. Requirements vary by county.
