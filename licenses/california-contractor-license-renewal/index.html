California Contractor License Renewal: Deadline, Cost, and Requirements (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: California LLCs

California contractor licenses are renewed every two years through the Contractors State License Board (CSLB). At $450, it is one of the more expensive license renewals. California does not require continuing education for contractors — but you must maintain your bond and insurance.

When is it due?

Your license expires 2 years from issuance. CSLB mails renewal notices about 60 days before expiration.

How much does it cost?

Renewal is $450 for active licenses and $225 for inactive. You can renew late for up to 5 years by paying all back renewal fees.

How to file

Filing steps

Verify your contractor bond is current ($25,000 minimum)
Verify workers compensation insurance (if you have employees)
Go to CSLB website
Submit renewal application and pay $450
Save confirmation

What happens if you miss the deadline?

You can renew a lapsed license for up to 5 years by paying all missed renewal fees. After 5 years, you must re-apply and retest.

Do you need a registered agent in California?

No CE Required: California does not require continuing education for contractor license renewal. However, you must maintain a $25,000 contractor bond and workers compensation insurance if you have employees.

Need a registered agent in California?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Ongoing requirements for California LLCs

Maintain $25,000 contractor bond

Submit renewal to CSLB
Pay $450 renewal fee
Update any business or personnel changes

Frequently asked questions

Does California require CE for contractors?

No. California is one of the few states with no CE requirement for contractor license renewal.

What is the contractor bond?

A $25,000 surety bond required by CSLB. It protects consumers. You pay a premium (typically 1-3% or $250-$750/year) to a surety company.

Can I renew an expired license?

Yes, for up to 5 years by paying all missed renewal fees. After 5 years, start over.
