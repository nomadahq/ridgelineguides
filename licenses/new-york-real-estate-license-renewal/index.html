New York Real Estate License Renewal: CE Hours, Deadline, and Costs (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: New York LLCs

New York real estate licenses must be renewed every two years through the Department of State. Renewal requires 22.5 hours of continuing education, including mandatory courses on fair housing, agency, and ethics.

When is it due?

Your license expires 2 years from issuance. The Department of State sends renewal notices approximately 90 days before expiration. You can renew online.

How much does it cost?

Renewal fees are $55 for salespersons and $200 for brokers. CE course costs are separate, typically $100-$250.

How to file

Filing steps

Complete 22.5 hours of CE before expiration
Go to NY DOS website
Log in and submit renewal application
Pay the renewal fee
Save confirmation

What happens if you miss the deadline?

If your license expires, you cannot practice until you renew. Extended lapses may require retaking the licensing exam.

Do you need a registered agent in New York?

CE Requirements: 22.5 hours per 2-year cycle including: 3 hours fair housing/discrimination, 2 hours agency law, 1 hour ethics, 1 hour recent legal matters, and elective hours from approved providers.

Need a registered agent in New York?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Frequently asked questions

Can I take CE online?

Yes. New York accepts online CE from approved providers.

What if I just got my license?

First-time renewals require 75 hours of qualifying education (the salesperson course satisfies this).

Can I renew with an expired license?

Within a grace period, yes with additional requirements. After extended lapse, you may need to retake the exam.
