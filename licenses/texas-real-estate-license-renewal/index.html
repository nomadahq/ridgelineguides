Texas Real Estate License Renewal: CE Hours, Deadline, and Costs (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Texas LLCs

Texas real estate licenses must be renewed every two years through the Texas Real Estate Commission (TREC). Renewal requires completing continuing education (CE) hours and paying the renewal fee. If you miss your deadline, you can still renew late within 6 months with an additional fee. After 6 months, you must retake the licensing exam.

When is it due?

Your Texas real estate license renewal is due by your individual license expiration date, which is every two years from your original issuance or last renewal. TREC sends email reminders before the deadline. You can renew up to 90 days before expiration.

How much does it cost?

Renewal fees are $110 for active sales agents and $330 for brokers. These fees are paid directly to TREC during online renewal. CE course costs are separate and vary by provider — typically $50–$200 for all required hours.

How to file

Filing steps

Complete all required CE hours before your expiration date
Go to TREC website
Log in to your license holder account
Verify your CE hours have been reported by your CE provider
Pay the renewal fee ($110 for sales agents, $330 for brokers)
Print or save your updated license

What happens if you miss the deadline?

If you miss your expiration date, you have a 6-month grace period to renew late with additional fees. After 6 months of expiration, your license enters an inactive status and you must satisfy additional requirements including potentially retaking the state exam to reactivate.

Do you need a registered agent in Texas?

CE Requirements: Texas requires 18 hours of CE for each renewal cycle, including: 4 hours of Legal Update I, 4 hours of Legal Update II, and 10 hours of elective CE from TREC-approved providers. First-time renewals (after the first license period) require the full SAE course requirements — check TREC for details.

Need a registered agent in Texas?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Frequently asked questions

Where can I take CE courses?

TREC-approved CE providers include Champions School of Real Estate, Kaplan Real Estate Education, The CE Shop, and many others. Online courses are available and widely accepted.

What if my CE hours are not showing up in TREC's system?

CE providers are required to report your completion to TREC within 10 days. If your hours do not appear, contact your CE provider first. You can also manually report completion to TREC with your certificate.

Can I renew with an inactive license?

If your license has been expired for less than 6 months, you can renew late with additional fees. If expired for more than 6 months but less than 2 years, you may need to complete additional education. After 2 years, you typically need to start the licensing process over.
