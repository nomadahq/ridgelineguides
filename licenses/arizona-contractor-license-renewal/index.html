Arizona Contractor License Renewal: Deadline, Cost, and Requirements (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Arizona LLCs

Arizona contractor licenses are renewed biennially through the Registrar of Contractors (ROC). Arizona does not require continuing education for general contractor renewal, but you must maintain your bond and insurance.

When is it due?

Your license expires 2 years from issuance. ROC sends renewal notices before expiration.

How much does it cost?

Renewal fees range from $300-$500 depending on license classification. Bond costs are separate.

How to file

Filing steps

Verify your contractor bond is current
Verify liability insurance is current
Go to ROC website
Submit renewal application
Pay the renewal fee
Save confirmation

What happens if you miss the deadline?

Late renewal is possible with additional fees. Extended non-renewal may require re-examination.

Do you need a registered agent in Arizona?

No CE Required: Arizona does not require continuing education for contractor license renewal. However, you must maintain your surety bond and liability insurance.

Need a registered agent in Arizona?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Arizona LLC compliance checklist

Ongoing requirements for Arizona LLCs

Maintain contractor surety bond
Maintain liability insurance
Submit renewal to ROC
Pay renewal fee ($300-$500)
Update any business information changes

Frequently asked questions

Does Arizona require CE?

No. Arizona is one of the states with no CE requirement for contractor renewal.

What bond amount is required?

Bond requirements vary by license classification and volume of work. Check with ROC for your specific requirement.

Can I renew online?

Yes. Arizona ROC accepts online renewals.
