Georgia Real Estate License Renewal: CE Hours, Deadline, and Costs (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Georgia LLCs

Georgia real estate licenses are renewed every four years through the Georgia Real Estate Commission (GREC). The renewal requires 36 hours of continuing education — including a mandatory 3-hour course on license law.

When is it due?

Your license expires 4 years from issuance. GREC sends renewal notices before expiration. You can renew online or by mail.

How much does it cost?

Renewal fees are $125 for salespersons and $170 for brokers. CE course costs are separate.

How to file

Filing steps

Complete 36 hours of CE
Go to GREC website
Submit renewal application
Pay the renewal fee
Save confirmation

What happens if you miss the deadline?

Late renewal is available within 60 days of expiration with an additional fee. After that, reinstatement requires additional steps.

Do you need a registered agent in Georgia?

CE Requirements: 36 hours per 4-year cycle including: 3 hours of Georgia license law (mandatory), 3 hours of a Commission-selected course, and 30 hours of elective CE.

Need a registered agent in Georgia?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Georgia LLC compliance checklist

Ongoing requirements for Georgia LLCs

Complete 36 hours of CE over the 4-year cycle
Include 3 hours Georgia license law
Include 3 hours Commission-selected course
Submit renewal to GREC
Pay renewal fee

Frequently asked questions

Why is the cycle 4 years?

Georgia uses a longer renewal cycle than most states, which is why the CE requirement (36 hours) is higher.

Can I take CE online?

Yes. GREC approves many online CE providers.

When should I start CE courses?

You can spread your 36 hours over the full 4-year cycle. Do not wait until the last minute.
