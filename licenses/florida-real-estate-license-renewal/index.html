Florida Real Estate License Renewal: CE Hours, Deadline, and Costs (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Florida LLCs

Florida real estate licenses are renewed every two years through the Department of Business and Professional Regulation (DBPR). The renewal requires completing 14 hours of continuing education and paying the renewal fee. Florida has one of the lower renewal fees in the country.

When is it due?

Florida real estate licenses expire either on March 31 or September 30 of the renewal year, depending on when you were originally licensed. Renewal opens 90 days before expiration. DBPR sends email reminders.

How much does it cost?

Renewal fees are $32 for sales associates and $36 for brokers, paid to DBPR during online renewal. CE course costs are separate — typically $30–$100 for all required hours.

How to file

Filing steps

Complete 14 hours of CE before your expiration date
Go to myfloridalicense.com
Log in to your account
Submit renewal application and verify CE completion
Pay the renewal fee
Print or save your renewed license

What happens if you miss the deadline?

If your license expires, you have 12 months to renew late. During this period, you cannot practice. Late renewal requires additional CE hours and fees. After 12 months, your license becomes null and you must re-apply and pass the exam again.

Do you need a registered agent in Florida?

CE Requirements: Florida requires 14 hours of CE per renewal cycle, including: 3 hours of core law, 3 hours of ethics, and 8 hours of specialty CE from FREC-approved providers. First-time renewals require a 45-hour post-licensing course instead of standard CE.

Need a registered agent in Florida?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Florida LLC compliance checklist

Ongoing requirements for Florida LLCs

Complete 14 hours of CE (3 hr core law + 3 hr ethics + 8 hr specialty)
Complete 45-hour post-licensing course if this is your first renewal
Log in to myfloridalicense.com and submit renewal
Pay renewal fee ($32 sales associate / $36 broker)
Print or save renewed license

Frequently asked questions

What is the post-licensing requirement?

If this is your first renewal (initial license period), you must complete a 45-hour post-licensing course instead of the standard 14 hours of CE. This applies to both sales associates and brokers.

Can I take CE courses online?

Yes. Florida accepts online CE courses from FREC-approved providers. Popular options include Gold Coast Schools, Larson Educational Services, and The CE Shop.

What happens if I miss the renewal deadline by more than 12 months?

Your license becomes null and void. You would need to re-apply for licensure and pass the state exam again.
