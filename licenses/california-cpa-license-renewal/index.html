California CPA License Renewal: CPE Hours and Deadline (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: California LLCs

California CPAs must renew their license every two years with the California Board of Accountancy (CBA). Renewal requires 80 hours of continuing professional education (CPE) per 2-year cycle, with specific requirements for ethics, fraud, and regulatory review.

When is it due?

Your renewal is due by the last day of your birth month, every two years. CBA assigns whether you renew in odd or even years.

How much does it cost?

The renewal fee is $280. CPE course costs are additional.

How to file

Filing steps

Complete 80 hours of CPE
Go to CBA website
Submit renewal application
Attest to CPE compliance
Pay $280 renewal fee

What happens if you miss the deadline?

Late renewal is possible with penalty fees. Extended delinquency requires additional steps for reinstatement.

Do you need a registered agent in California?

CPE Requirements: 80 hours per 2-year cycle with minimum 20 hours per year. Must include: 4 hours ethics, 2 hours fraud, 2 hours California regulatory review. Must include subjects directly related to accounting practice.

Need a registered agent in California?

A registered agent keeps your LLC in good standing and ensures you receive all legal and state documents on time.

Submit renewal to CBA
Pay $280 fee
Retain CPE records for 5 years

Frequently asked questions

Is peer review required?

Peer review is required for CPAs who perform attest services (audits, reviews). It is separate from the license renewal CPE requirement.

Can I take CPE online?

Yes. CBA accepts CPE from NASBA-registered providers, including online courses.

What if I am not practicing?

You can place your license in inactive status to avoid CPE requirements, but cannot use the CPA designation while inactive.
