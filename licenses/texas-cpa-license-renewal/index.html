Texas CPA License Renewal: CPE Hours and Deadline (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Texas LLCs

Texas CPAs must renew their license annually with the Texas State Board of Public Accountancy (TSBPA). The renewal requires paying the annual fee and meeting continuing professional education (CPE) requirements over a rolling 3-year cycle. Texas requires 120 hours of CPE per 3-year period, with a minimum of 20 hours per year.

When is it due?

Your Texas CPA license renewal is due by the last day of your birth month each year. TSBPA sends renewal reminders. You can renew online up to 60 days before your deadline.

How much does it cost?

The annual renewal fee is $60. CPE course costs vary by provider — expect $200–$1,000+ per year depending on how you fulfill your hours.

How to file

Filing steps

Ensure you have met CPE requirements (minimum 20 hours for the current year)
Go to TSBPA website
Log in to your CPA license account
Complete the renewal application and attest to CPE compliance
Pay the $60 renewal fee
Keep CPE certificates on file for at least 5 years

What happens if you miss the deadline?

If you miss your renewal deadline, the board will assess late fees and may suspend your license. During suspension, you cannot practice public accounting or use the CPA designation. Reinstatement requires paying all outstanding fees and demonstrating CPE compliance.

Do you need a registered agent in Texas?

CPE Requirements: Texas requires 120 hours of CPE per rolling 3-year cycle, with a minimum of 20 hours per year. At least 2 hours of ethics CPE are required each year. CPE must be from TSBPA-approved providers or NASBA-registered sponsors.

Frequently asked questions

What counts as CPE?

CPE includes seminars, conferences, webinars, self-study courses, and university courses related to accounting, auditing, tax, and other CPA competency areas. Courses must be from TSBPA-approved providers.

Can I carry over excess CPE hours?

Excess hours completed in one year can be applied toward the 3-year rolling requirement, but you still must meet the 20-hour annual minimum each year.

What if I am not practicing?

You can place your license in inactive or retired status to avoid CPE requirements. However, you cannot use the CPA designation or practice public accounting while inactive.
