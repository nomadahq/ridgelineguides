Business Insurance Deduction: What Premiums Are Deductible? (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Self-employed individuals and small business owners

Business insurance premiums are deductible as ordinary and necessary business expenses on Schedule C. This includes general liability, professional liability (E&O), property insurance, business interruption insurance, and more. If you pay for insurance to protect your business, it is almost certainly deductible.

Quick summary

All insurance premiums for business protection are deductible on Schedule C. This includes liability, professional, property, cyber, and business vehicle insurance. Health insurance for self-employed individuals has a separate, specific deduction (Schedule 1, not Schedule C).

How the deduction works

Business insurance premiums are deducted as ordinary business expenses on Schedule C, Line 15. The insurance must be for business purposes — personal insurance is not deductible here. If a policy covers both business and personal use (like a vehicle), deduct only the business-use portion.

What qualifies

  • General liability insurance
  • Professional liability / Errors & Omissions (E&O)
  • Business property insurance
  • Business interruption insurance
  • Cyber liability insurance
  • Commercial auto insurance (business-use portion)
  • Workers compensation insurance
  • Product liability insurance
  • Directors and officers (D&O) insurance
  • Business umbrella policy

What does not qualify

  • Personal health insurance (separate deduction on Schedule 1)
  • Personal auto insurance (business portion may be deductible)
  • Life insurance where you are the beneficiary
  • Personal property insurance
  • Self-insurance reserves (not deductible until actually paid)

How to calculate

Add up all business insurance premiums paid during the year. For policies covering both business and personal use, apply the business-use percentage. Deduct the total on Schedule C, Line 15 (Insurance).

Documentation you need

Keep these records

Insurance policy documents
Premium payment receipts or statements
Business-use percentage for mixed-use policies
Certificates of insurance

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Forgetting to deduct smaller policies (cyber, professional liability)
  • Not separating business health insurance from the general deduction (different tax treatment)
  • Missing the deduction for workers comp premiums
  • Not deducting the business portion of personal auto insurance
IRS audit note: Business insurance deductions are very low-risk for audits. Keep policy documents and payment records on file.

Frequently asked questions

Is health insurance deducted on Schedule C?

No. Self-employed health insurance is deducted on Schedule 1, Line 17 as an above-the-line deduction, not as a business expense on Schedule C. This matters because the Schedule 1 deduction reduces AGI.

Can I deduct cyber insurance?

Yes. Cyber liability insurance premiums are deductible as a business expense.

What about workers comp?

Workers compensation insurance premiums are 100% deductible as a business expense on Schedule C.
