Professional Development and Education Deduction (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Self-employed individuals investing in professional development

If you invest in courses, certifications, conferences, books, or coaching to maintain or improve skills in your current business, those costs are deductible. The key requirement is that the education must relate to your existing business — not qualify you for a new profession.

Quick summary

Education that maintains or improves skills in your current business is deductible on Schedule C. This includes courses, certifications, conferences, books, coaching, and professional memberships. Education that qualifies you for a new career is NOT deductible as a business expense.

How the deduction works

Professional development expenses are deducted as ordinary business expenses on Schedule C. The education must either maintain or improve skills required in your current business, or be required by law or regulation to keep your current license or professional standing. Education that qualifies you for a new trade or business is not deductible, even if it also improves your current skills.

What qualifies

  • Online courses related to your business
  • Professional certifications and renewal fees
  • Industry conferences and seminars
  • Business books, audiobooks, and publications
  • Coaching or consulting fees
  • Professional association memberships
  • Webinars and workshops
  • Continuing education credits required for your license

What does not qualify

  • Education to qualify for a new career
  • College degrees (unless required to maintain current position)
  • Courses unrelated to your current business
  • Personal development courses with no business connection

How to calculate

Add up all qualifying professional development expenses for the year. Deduct the full amount on Schedule C. Travel to conferences includes additional deductions for airfare, hotel, and meals (50%) under the business travel rules.

Documentation you need

Keep these records

Receipts for courses, books, conferences, and memberships
Course descriptions showing relevance to your business
Conference agendas and materials
Records of CE credits earned

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Not deducting books and online courses (small amounts add up)
  • Claiming education for a new career as a business expense
  • Forgetting to deduct professional association dues
  • Not deducting travel expenses related to conferences separately
  • Missing the deduction for required CE credits
IRS audit note: Professional development deductions are generally low-risk. Keep course descriptions to show business relevance if questioned.

Frequently asked questions

Can I deduct an MBA?

Generally no, unless you are already in a management role and the MBA maintains or improves your current skills. If the MBA qualifies you for a new career, it is not deductible.

Are online course platforms deductible?

Yes. Subscriptions to platforms like LinkedIn Learning, Coursera, Udemy, or MasterClass are deductible if the courses relate to your business.

Can I deduct books?

Yes. Business books, audiobooks, and industry publications are deductible as professional development expenses.
