SEP IRA Deduction: Contribution Limits and Rules for Self-Employed (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Self-employed individuals, freelancers, sole proprietors, small business owners

A SEP IRA (Simplified Employee Pension Individual Retirement Account) is one of the most powerful tax-saving tools available to self-employed individuals. It allows you to contribute up to 25% of your net self-employment income — up to $70,000 in 2025 — and deduct the entire contribution from your taxable income. It is easy to set up, has no annual filing requirements, and contributions are flexible from year to year.

Quick summary

Contribute up to 25% of net self-employment income, maximum $70,000 (2025 limit). Contributions are fully tax-deductible. No annual filing requirements. You can open and fund a SEP IRA up until your tax filing deadline (including extensions).

How the SEP IRA works

A SEP IRA is a retirement account that lets self-employed individuals make tax-deductible contributions based on their income. The contribution limit is 25% of net self-employment earnings (after the deduction for self-employment tax). Contributions reduce your taxable income dollar-for-dollar. The money grows tax-deferred until withdrawal in retirement. You can open and fund a SEP IRA as late as your tax filing deadline, including extensions — meaning you can make a 2025 contribution as late as October 15, 2026 if you file an extension.

What qualifies

  • Contributions up to 25% of net self-employment income
  • Maximum contribution of $70,000 (2025) or $69,000 (2024)
  • Contributions made by the tax filing deadline (including extensions)
  • Employer contributions (if you have employees, you must contribute equally for them)

What does not qualify

  • Contributions exceeding 25% of net self-employment income
  • Contributions exceeding the annual dollar limit
  • Employee elective deferrals (SEP IRAs are employer-contribution only)
  • Roth contributions (SEP IRAs are traditional/pre-tax only)
  • Catch-up contributions (SEP IRAs do not have catch-up provisions)

How to calculate your maximum contribution

The calculation for self-employed individuals is slightly different from the 25% rule for employees. For sole proprietors: (1) Start with net self-employment income (Schedule C profit minus half of self-employment tax). (2) Multiply by 20% (not 25% — the effective rate is lower for sole proprietors due to the way the deduction interacts with self-employment income). The result is your maximum contribution. Example: $100,000 net Schedule C profit → roughly $18,587 maximum SEP IRA contribution after the self-employment tax adjustment.

Documentation you need

Keep these records

SEP IRA plan document (provided by your brokerage when you open the account)
Records of contributions made each year
Schedule C showing net self-employment income
Proof of deposit dates (contributions must be made by filing deadline)

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Contributing more than the allowable limit (the effective rate for sole proprietors is about 20%, not 25%)
  • Missing the contribution deadline (must be made by tax filing deadline including extensions)
  • Not knowing that if you have employees, you must contribute the same percentage for them
  • Confusing SEP IRA with SIMPLE IRA or Solo 401(k) — different rules and limits
  • Forgetting to actually deduct the contribution on your tax return (Schedule 1, Line 16)
IRS audit note: SEP IRA contributions are straightforward and not a high-audit-risk item. The main risks are exceeding the contribution limit or failing to contribute for eligible employees. Keep records of your calculation and contribution dates.

Frequently asked questions

SEP IRA vs Solo 401(k) — which is better?

A Solo 401(k) allows both employer and employee contributions, which can result in a higher total contribution at lower income levels. A SEP IRA is simpler to set up and administer. At higher income levels (above ~$200k), the maximums converge. If you want Roth contributions or loan provisions, the Solo 401(k) is the only option.

Can I open a SEP IRA after the tax year ends?

Yes. You can open and fund a SEP IRA up until your tax filing deadline, including extensions. This means you can make your 2025 contribution as late as October 15, 2026 if you file an extension.

Do I have to contribute every year?

No. SEP IRA contributions are flexible — you can contribute different amounts each year or skip years entirely. There is no minimum annual contribution requirement.
