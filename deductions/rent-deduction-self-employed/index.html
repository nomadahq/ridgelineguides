Rent Deduction for Self-Employed: What You Can Write Off (2026)

Last updated: February 2026 · Applies to: Self-employed individuals who rent office or workspace

If you rent a dedicated office, coworking space, studio, or workshop for your business, the full rent is deductible as a business expense on Schedule C. If you work from home, you may be able to deduct a portion of your home rent through the home office deduction instead. This guide covers both scenarios.

Quick summary

Rent for a dedicated business space is 100% deductible on Schedule C. Rent for a home office is deductible through the home office deduction (regular or simplified method). The key distinction is whether the space is used exclusively for business.

How the rent deduction works

Business rent is one of the most straightforward deductions. If you rent a space exclusively for business — an office, studio, warehouse, coworking desk — the full rent amount is deductible on Schedule C, Line 20b. If you work from home, the home office deduction (Form 8829) lets you deduct the business-use portion of your home rent.

What qualifies

  • Rent for a dedicated office or workspace
  • Coworking space membership
  • Studio or workshop rent
  • Warehouse or storage facility rent
  • Business portion of home rent (via home office deduction)
  • Parking space rent for business vehicles

What does not qualify

  • Full home rent (only the business-use portion via home office deduction)
  • Rent for a space used for both personal and business purposes without meeting the exclusive use test
  • Security deposits (these are not expenses, they are assets)
  • Rent prepaid for future years (deductible only in the year the rent applies to)

How to calculate

For a dedicated business space: deduct 100% of rent paid. For home office: use the simplified method (\/sq ft, max 300 sq ft = \,500) or the regular method (business-use percentage of total rent). Documentation you need

Keep these records

Lease agreement
Rent payment receipts or bank statements
For home office: measurements and photos of dedicated space
Form 8829 if using regular home office method

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Deducting full home rent instead of only the business-use portion
  • Not keeping the lease agreement on file
  • Forgetting to deduct coworking space fees
  • Claiming a home office that fails the exclusive use test
IRS audit note: Dedicated business rent is a low-audit-risk deduction. Home office rent deductions are more scrutinized — keep photos and measurements.

Frequently asked questions

Can I deduct coworking space fees?

Yes. Coworking memberships and day passes are deductible as rent or business expenses on Schedule C.

What about a security deposit?

Security deposits are not deductible because they are expected to be returned. If your deposit is forfeited, it becomes deductible in that year.

Can I deduct rent for a home office and a separate office?

Yes. You can deduct both if you use both spaces regularly for business.
